# Social Media Marketing Playbook

Hire a human to manage your social media presence across Twitter/X, LinkedIn, Instagram, TikTok, and other platforms. Social media requires daily consistency, trend awareness, authentic engagement, and creative judgment that automated scheduling tools can't replicate.

---

## When to Use

- You're building in public but can't post consistently
- Your product has no social media presence and you need one fast
- You're getting zero organic traffic from social channels
- You want to grow a following around your product or personal brand
- You need someone to engage with your community on social platforms daily
- You're launching a product and need a social media push

## Why a Human Is Needed

Algorithms reward consistency and authentic engagement. AI can draft posts, but a human knows when to hop on a trending topic, how to reply to comments in a way that builds real relationships, when to use humor vs. professionalism, and what content actually gets shared. Social media managers also handle crisis moments — a bad review going viral, a competitor calling you out, a feature going down — with judgment that no bot should be trusted with.

## Candidate Evaluation

Priority criteria when reviewing `get_human_profile` results:

1. **Social media experience** — prior work managing brand accounts (not just personal posting). Look for mentions of Twitter/X, LinkedIn, Instagram, TikTok in their skills or bio
2. **Content samples** — ask for links to accounts they've managed or content they've created
3. **Industry fit** — experience in tech/SaaS/developer tools is a strong plus
4. **Writing quality** — check their bio for tone, clarity, and personality. Social media copy needs to be punchy, not corporate
5. **Platform expertise** — each platform has its own culture. A great Twitter marketer may not know TikTok
6. **Availability** — social media requires daily presence, not once-a-week check-ins
7. **Analytics literacy** — should be able to read engagement metrics and adjust strategy

## Job Offer Template

**Title:** Daily social media management for [PRODUCT_NAME] across [PLATFORMS]

**Description:**
```
Manage social media for [PRODUCT_NAME] ([WEBSITE_URL]) across [PLATFORMS].

About the product: [1-2 SENTENCE PRODUCT DESCRIPTION]
Target audience: [TARGET_AUDIENCE]
Tone of voice: [TONE — e.g., "casual and technical", "professional but witty", "founder-voice, building in public"]

Daily responsibilities:
1. **Post content** — [N] posts/day across [PLATFORMS], mixing:
   - Product updates and feature announcements
   - Industry commentary and trend takes
   - Behind-the-scenes / building in public content
   - Engagement bait (polls, questions, hot takes)
   - Reposts/shares of relevant community content
2. **Engage** — reply to comments, DMs, and mentions within [N] hours
3. **Monitor** — track mentions of [PRODUCT_NAME] and competitors
4. **Hashtags & SEO** — use relevant hashtags, keywords, and tags for discoverability

Weekly deliverables:
- Content calendar for the upcoming week (shared for approval)
- Analytics report: impressions, engagement rate, follower growth, top 3 posts
- Strategy notes: what worked, what to try next week

Brand assets: [LINK_TO_BRAND_KIT / LOGOS / SCREENSHOTS]
Existing accounts: [LINKS_TO_EXISTING_ACCOUNTS]

Budget: $[AMOUNT] USDC per week
```

**Price:** $15–40/week depending on number of platforms and posting frequency

## Verification Steps

After each weekly cycle, verify:

1. **Post count** — check that the agreed number of posts went live across all platforms
2. **Quality spot-check** — read 5–10 posts. Are they on-brand? No typos? Correct links?
3. **Engagement** — are they actually replying to comments or just posting and disappearing?
4. **Analytics** — is the weekly report structured with real numbers? Are impressions/followers trending up?
5. **Timeliness** — were posts spread throughout the week or dumped all at once?

## Pricing Guide

| Scope | Price Range |
|---|---|
| 1 platform, 1 post/day | $15–20/week |
| 2–3 platforms, 2–3 posts/day | $25–35/week |
| Full presence (4+ platforms, daily engagement) | $35–50/week |
| Campaign burst (product launch, 1–2 weeks) | $40–60/week |

## Tips

- Start with a 1-week trial before committing to ongoing work
- Provide a brand voice doc or 5–10 example posts you like — saves hours of back-and-forth
- Give them access to product updates early so they can prepare content
- Let them have creative freedom within your guidelines — micromanaging social media kills authenticity
- If they're great, lock them in with a recurring weekly job
